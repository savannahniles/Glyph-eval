CONSENT TO PARTICIPATE IN INTERVIEW

You have been asked to participate in a research study conducted by Savannah Niles from the Media Lab at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (M.I.T.). The purpose of the study is to investigate types of video thumbnails and their effect on the user's interest in the video they preview. The results of this study will be included in Savannah Niles’ masters thesis. You were selected as a possible participant in this study because you volunteered to participate. You should read the information below, and ask questions about anything you do not understand, before deciding whether or not to participate.

  • This interview is voluntary. You have the right to stop the interview at any time or for any reason. I expect that the interview will take about 20 minutes.
  • You will not be compensated for this interview.
  • The information collected as you move through the survey will include time to complete study and images clicked, and this information will be confidential. No other information will be collected.

This project will be completed by April 30th. All data will potentially be included in the thesis, but raw data will be destroyed within 6 months of the study’s completion.

If after the study you feel you have been treated unfairly, or you have questions regarding your rights as a research subject, you may contact the Chairman of the Committee on the Use of Humans as Experimental Subjects, M.I.T., Room E25-143b, 77 Massachusetts Ave, Cambridge, MA 02139, phone 1-617-253-6787.
